Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is Section 80G of the Income Tax Act?
Section 80G of the Income Tax Act, 1961, allows you to claim deductions for donations made to eligible charitable organizations. This means you can reduce your taxable income by the amount you donate, lowering your overall tax liability.
2. Is there a limit on 80G tax deductions?
Yes, there are limits. For some donations, you can claim 100% of the donated amount, while for others, it's 50%. There's also an overall limit of 10% of your 'Adjusted Gross Total Income'. The exact deduction depends on the charity's registration.
3. Why should I donate under Section 80G?
Donating under Section 80G offers a double benefit. You get the personal satisfaction of supporting a cause you care about, and you also receive a direct financial benefit by reducing your income tax payment. It's a powerful way to make your contribution go further.
4. Can Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) claim 80G benefits?
Absolutely. NRIs who have taxable income in India can claim deductions under Section 80G for donations made to eligible Indian charities. The process is the same as for resident Indians.
5. How do I claim the 80G deduction?
To claim the deduction, you must have a donation receipt and a Form 10BE certificate issued by the charitable organization. The NGO will typically email you this certificate. When filing your income tax return (ITR), you need to provide the details from this certificate.
