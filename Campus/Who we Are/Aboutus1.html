About KASH Campus

Solidarity for Developing communities (SOLID), a registered society, established in the year of 1993 at Berhampur, Odisha. SOLID started its foray into education way back in 1993 when they adopted Anganwadis in the village of Purushottampur, and today they boast an all-inclusive campus in the last 29 years of Operation. The dawn of the Education awareness in Higher Education ushered in the horizon of KASH Project Campus, a cultured & beautiful area in the District of Ganjam by swearing the opening of the college in the name of KARUNASHANTI (KASH) Science residential Project Campus, by sowing the provision of +2 Science Residential Campus. The college was inaugurated in 2003. Now it is running its 20th eventful year of life by sufficing Higher educational needs to the Adivasis, Dalits and OBC Students from the Project areas of Ganjam, Gajapati, Kandhamal, & Rayagada.

Late Shri. Bijayananda Singh, the founder of the Programme Project campus, is the first, founding Secretary and the present permanent late-emeritus president, a dedicated social worker and a keen educationist with his sharp academic acumen, futuristic vision, the strong, impartial, transparent and able leadership has guided this Institution to its present state of fame and glory. Late Shri Bijayananda Singh a man of strong and clear convictions endowed with keen sense of imagination and dedication had been serving as a Member in the Programme Project Campus Council and the Programme Project Campus Committee and has been the guiding light behind the growth of the institution on the right path. Mr. Debasish Pattnaik, the Current Secretary and Executive Director, is a strong achiever who has taken over charge as the Secretary and Executive Director of the NGO SOLID and KASH at present.

The Project Campus is ideally located in serene and rural surroundings not far from the city. It is conveniently connected with all parts of the city through frequent town buses plying from various destinations. Started in the year 2003 with a humble beginning, the Programme Project Campus has grown magnificently in academic stature. The Programme Project Campus is imparting higher education to approximately 5000 students, in the last 19 years, majority of whom hail from marginalized, socially and economically weaker sections of the society, specifically from tribal and Dalit communities. More than 40% of the students studying in the Programme Project Campus are girls and thus the Programme Project Campus is playing a pivotal role in women empowerment and social justice. At present, approximately 256 students are pursuing their higher secondary education in the KASH Campus. It provides students an environment for overall development, nurturing them for a successful career, and contributing to the betterment of society with a sustainable future for the entire family.
